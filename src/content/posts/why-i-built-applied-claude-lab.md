Look, I'll be honest — I almost didn't write this.

Not because I had nothing to say, but because I wasn't sure if anyone would care. Then I remembered: I'm writing this for me first, and if you find it useful, that's a bonus.

## Who Am I

I'm a software and hardware engineer. My day job involves real engineering work — systems, automation, integration, the kind of stuff where "just use AI" is both the funniest and most frustrating advice you can get.

Over the past year or so, I've been going deep on AI tools — not as a side hobby, but as something I'm actively embedding into my actual work. I've implemented AI solutions for my team. I've led engineers — both SW and HW — through the awkward "wait, I can do *what* with this?" phase. I've made mistakes, wasted tokens, gotten genuinely surprised by what works, and learned what doesn't.

This site is where I put all of that down.

## What We Actually Do Here

The name says AI, but the content is really about *how engineers use AI* — practically, daily, without the hype.

That means:

- How to wire AI into real engineering workflows (not just chatting with a bot)
- Test automation with **OpenTAP** and how Claude fits in
- Prompt engineering that produces *deterministic, repeatable* output — not vibes
- Leading your team to use AI without it becoming a circus
- Using **Claude** as a primary tool — but also Codex, and whatever else earns a spot in the toolkit

No gatekeeping. If a tool works, it works.

## The Elephant in the Room

*"Are you using AI to write this content?"*

Fair question. Short answer: **the ideas, opinions, and war stories are 100% mine** — stuff I actually lived, thought about, or screwed up. The writing? Sometimes I'll run a draft through Claude or another tool to clean up grammar or structure.

Because nothing kills your credibility faster than a typo in a technical post. Grammar mistakes cause grammar cancer.

![Grammar mistakes cause grammar cancer](/images/grammar-cancer-meme.png)

And yes, I see the irony of an AI engineer using AI to fix his grammar. I'm choosing to live with it.

## Why Now

I've spent time showing engineers at work how to use AI effectively. At some point it made sense to write it down somewhere that wasn't a Teams chat that disappears in six months.

So here we are.

New posts as I have something real to share — not on a schedule, not for the algorithm. When it's worth your time, it'll show up.

Let's build.
